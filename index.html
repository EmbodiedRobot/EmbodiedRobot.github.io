Empowering Embodied Manipulation: A Bimanual-Mobile Robot Manipulation Dataset for Household Tasks

JD Explore Academy

Abstract

As Embodied AI advances, the deployment of robots with dual-arm and mobile capabilities is increasingly enhancing the complexity and efficiency of interactions in household environments. Despite their potential, the development of these robots is constrained by the lack of dual-arm robot manipulation datasets that reflect the intricacy of real-world household tasks. To address this gap, we introduce BRMData, a Bimanual-mobile Robot Manipulation Dataset tailored for household applications. This dataset is collected using an advanced mobile robot manipulation platform equipped with RGBD sensing and a teleoperation system featuring four collaborative robot arms, enabling a wide range of manipulation tasks. We have captured data for 10 different household tasks, including single-arm, dual-arm, and mobile manipulations, with progressively increasing difficulty and layered task structures. BRMData encompasses human-robot interactions and dynamic mobile manipulations, closely simulating real-world household applications. With multi-view and depth-sensing data, BRMData aims to drive the development of adaptable and versatile robot manipulation technologies. This dataset is now open-sourced and available at this link, contributing further to advancements in the field.
