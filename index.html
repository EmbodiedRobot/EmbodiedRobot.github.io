BRMData: A Bimanual-Mobile Robot Manipulation Dataset

JD Explore Academy

Abstract

As Embodied AI progresses, the deployment of humanoid robots equipped with both dual-arm and mobile capabilities is increasingly enhancing the complexity and efficiency of interactions across various environments. Despite their potential, the development of these robots is limited by a lack of dual-arm robot manipulation datasets that mirror the complexity of real-world tasks. To bridge this gap, we introduce BRMData, a comprehensive Bimanual-mobile Robot Manipulation Dataset. This dataset is collected using a sophisticated mobile robot manipulation platform that features RGBD sensing and a teleoperation system with four collaborative robot arms, facilitating a wide range of manipulation tasks. From single-arm object handling to complex dual-arm scenarios, BRMData integrates both human-robot interactions and dynamic mobile manipulations, closely simulating real-world applications. With multi-view and cross-sensor data spanning 10 diverse manipulation tasks, BRMData is set to catalyze the development of adaptable and versatile robot manipulation technologies. This dataset is now open-sourced and available at this link, further contributing to advancements in the field.
